Model-Agnostic Meta-Learning (MAML)

Research Paper
Finn et al. (2017)
Core Method

MAML enables rapid adaptation to new tasks with minimal gradient steps, making it ideal for few-shot unity mathematics discovery across different mathematical domains.

MAML Algorithm:

1. Initialize parameters θ
2. Sample tasks Ti from task distribution
3. For each task: θ'i = θ - α∇θLTi(fθ)
4. Update: θ = θ - β∇θ∑iLTi(fθ'i)
Unity Mathematics Application: MAML can quickly adapt to recognize unity patterns (1+1=1, A∪A=A, max(a,a)=a) across different mathematical contexts with minimal training examples.

Gödel's Incompleteness Theorems

Fundamental Result
Kurt Gödel (1931)
Theoretical Foundation

Demonstrates that any consistent formal system containing arithmetic has true statements that cannot be proven within the system, showing inherent limitations of formal mathematical systems.

The Theorems:

First Theorem: Any consistent formal system F within which a certain amount of elementary arithmetic can be carried out is incomplete.
Second Theorem: No consistent formal system can prove its own consistency.
Unity Mathematics Connection: Unity mathematics may transcend traditional formal systems through consciousness-mediated proof validation, potentially operating beyond Gödelian limitations.
